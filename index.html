Your plan

from here to next review

Priority actions

  • Continue Berbersorb + Lipotropex unchanged — insulin, cholesterol and liver have all improved on them.
  • Switch vitamin D to the activated capsule (Opti Active D3 K2) — the spray isn't absorbing.
  • Start activated folate (Folinic Acid) — homocysteine confirms the methylation cycle needs it.
  • Continue breathing retraining daily — this is what addresses the bicarbonate result.
  • Adrenal support — waiting on DUTCH before adding anything.
  • Ask your GP about a parathyroid (PTH) test or DEXA scan — reading the bone picture behind your ALP.

Lifestyle

  • Breathing retraining — under 10 breaths/min (aim ~7), 15 min, 5×/week. Conditions the nervous system, supports bicarbonate, eases anxiety.
  • Evening wind-down — screens off from 8pm, consistent sleep and wake times to anchor cortisol rhythm (helps night waking and grinding).
  • Resistance training 2–3×/week + daily walking — lifts insulin sensitivity more than cardio alone; a 10-min walk after meals uses glucose without an insulin spike.
  • Blood-sugar-balanced meals — protein, vegetables and good fats; ease back on refined carbs and sugar.
  • Reduce alcohol — protects the liver gains and keeps ferritin down.
  • Hydration & minerals — steady through the day; ½ tsp sea salt in a little water after brushing at night. Continue mouth taping.

Your genetics

why we chose these forms
Methylation & nutrient SNPs ›

These variants shape how you absorb and use B-vitamins and choline — they're the reason your plan uses activated forms rather than standard ones. Set for life, so we read them once and let them guide every round.

MTHFR C677T

Activates folate into its usable form. Reduced activity can lift homocysteine — favour active folate. Fits your result exactly.

MTHFR A1298C

Side-branch of folate metabolism; milder alone, compounds when paired with 677.

COMT

Breaks down adrenaline, dopamine and oestrogen. A slower version means stress chemicals linger — relevant to anxiety, and why we introduce B-vitamins gently.

MTR & MTRR

Recycle B12 to keep methylation turning — they raise your need for adequate, usable B12.

PEMT

Makes phosphatidylcholine to export fat from the liver. A reduced version raises choline need and fatty-liver risk — relevant to your mild liver markers.

DHFR · MTHFD1 · FUT2 · CBS · TCN2 · AHCY · NBPF3

Together they steer folate/choline demand, B12 transport into cells, and sulphur balance — the full reason your forms and doses are chosen the way they are.
